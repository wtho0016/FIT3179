Where Everyone's Staying Home... Or Not

Domestic tourism patterns across countries in 2023

Domestic tourism tends to cluster in major cities and coastal areas such as the United Kingdom, China and India, showing how accessibility and infrastructure shape travel. Smaller or landlocked countries have more dispersed activity.

Peaks in travel often align with holiday seasons, with noticeable clustering in urban centers. Policy restrictions and public health measures also influence domestic tourism, explaining lower activity in regions like Africa.

Show Me the Money!

GDP of countries by Tourism in 2008, 2019, 2020, and 2021

2008

The 2008 financial crisis caused global economic turmoil, reducing disposable income and consumer confidence. This led to sharp declines in tourism as people cut back on travel spending, particularly affecting destinations reliant on international visitors.

2019

In 2019, global economic stability supported strong tourism growth, with rising disposable incomes fueling record international travel. However, this peak masked the unprecedented collapse that would follow in 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

2020

The COVID-19 pandemic triggered lockdowns and travel restrictions worldwide, causing massive drops in tourism. Countries reliant on inbound visitors experienced the sharpest declines.

2021

Tourism began recovering in 2021 with partial reopening of borders and vaccine rollouts. Domestic tourism remained stronger than international travel, showing uneven recovery across countries.

Where do Tourists Snooze?

Occupancy rates, room numbers, and average length of stay per night

Occupancy rates are generally higher in densely populated or highly touristic regions, suggesting that hotel demand closely follows urban hubs and popular destinations. Singapore constantly stands out with its near-full occupancy, reflecting the intensity of city-centered travel.

Average length of stay tends to be longer in remote or luxury destinations, indicating that some travelers prioritize immersive experiences over quick visits. French Polynesia exemplifies this, but overall the data suggests that geography, destination type, and lifestyle preferences shape how long tourists stay.
